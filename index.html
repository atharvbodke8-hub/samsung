Samsung mobile brands

Samsung Electronics is the world’s leading smartphone vendor, a position it has held for over a decade. As of 2025,
the company maintains a dominant global market share of approximately 19–20%, fueled by its flagship Galaxy S and Z series
and the popular mid-range Galaxy A series.
Core Mobile Information (2025)
1)Company Leadership: Jun Young-hyun is the sole leader of Samsung Electronics following the death of co-CEO Han Jong-hee in March 2025.
2)Galaxy AI Integration: The company focuses on expanding Galaxy AI across its lineup, including the Galaxy A series.
Key features include multimodal AI agents, context-aware searching, and "Gemini Live" integration.
3)Global Manufacturing: Samsung's largest mobile phone factory is in Noida, India.
The company also has production hubs in Vietnam.

Samsung laptop brands

Samsung's primary and current laptop line is the Samsung Galaxy Book series,
which replaced earlier product lines like the Samsung Notebook and ATIV Book series.
These laptops are known for their powerful performance, lightweight design, and seamless integration with the Samsung Galaxy ecosystem.

Current Samsung Laptop Series
1)Samsung's current offerings are primarily categorized under the Galaxy Book umbrella,
with different models catering to specific user needs, from everyday computing to high-performance tasks.
2)Samsung Galaxy Book (Base Model): The entry-level option in the lineup, designed for everyday computing needs with a focus on value,
portability, and solid performance for students and casual users.
3)Samsung Galaxy Book Pro: A core model designed with working professionals in mind, offering an ultra-thin
and lightweight form factor, powerful processors, and vibrant AMOLED displays.
4)Samsung Galaxy Book 360: These models feature a versatile 2-in-1 design with a 360-degree rotating hinge and S Pen stylus functionality,
allowing them to be used as both a traditional laptop and a tablet.
5)Samsung Galaxy Book Pro 360: Combines the premium features of the Pro line with the flexibility of the convertible 360-degree design and S Pen support,
appealing to users who need both performance and versatility.
6)Samsung Galaxy Book Ultra: The flagship, high-performance option tailored for demanding tasks like creative work, gaming, and heavy multitasking.
These models come equipped with powerful processors (e.g., Intel Core Ultra 7/9) and dedicated graphics cards (e.g., NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4050/4070).
7)Samsung Galaxy Book Edge: These are AI PCs running on ARM-based Snapdragon X Elite processors, highlighting next-gen AI performance and functionality.
8)Samsung Galaxy Chromebook: A separate line of laptops that runs on Google's Chrome OS instead of Microsoft Windows.
They are a great, budget-friendly option for users who primarily work online and use web-based applications.
Historical Laptop "Brands"
Over the years, Samsung has used several names for its laptops before consolidating under the Galaxy Book brand.
1)Samsung Sens: The original line of portable computers, launched in 1995 and lasting until 2013.
2)Samsung Ativ: The Ativ brand temporarily replaced the Sens line and was used for a range of Windows-based devices,
including the ATIV Book 9, ATIV Book 8, etc., before being dropped in favor of the more general "Samsung Notebook" branding.
3)Samsung Notebook: This branding was used for several years (e.g., Notebook 9, Notebook Odyssey for gaming, Notebook Plus)
until the Galaxy Book line became Samsung's main laptop offering around 2020/2021.
